Plastic Packaging

We are producing over 300 million tons of plastic every year, 50% of which is for single-use purposes – utilized for just a few moments, but on the planet for at least several hundred years.

Plastic pollution impacts sea turtles, whales, seabirds, fish, coral reefs, and countless other marine species and habitats. Scientists estimate that more than half of the world's sea turtles and nearly every seabird on Earth have eaten plastic in their lifetimes.

What makes a packaging material sustainable?

Efficient - The packaging line and machinery used are designed to utilize materials as efficiently as possible throughout the product lifecycle.
Cyclic - The materials used to package can be cycled through natural or technical systems to minimize degradation or the use of upgrading additives.
Safe - All parts of the packaging must not harmful to human health or the planet.
